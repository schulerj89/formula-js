# Announcer Dialogue

Formula JS uses two British-accent announcers: one race-craft lead and one dry comic color voice. Lines are concise for mobile subtitles, radio barks, and replay captions.

## Static Tokens

- `{player}`: player display name.
- `{track}`: current track name.
- Static racer names: `Luca Venn`, `Maya Cross`, `Theo Pike`, `Nina Vale`, `Arjun Stone`, `Bea Hart`, `Oscar Flint`.

## Characters

### Eleanor "Ellie" March

- Accent: crisp London / BBC sport, clipped under pressure.
- Role: lead announcer, race control feel, serious reads first.
- Personality: precise, competitive, notices tire strategy and line choice.
- Voice direction: controlled pace, low drama until a genuine moment, then sharp and urgent.
- Favorite phrasing: "clean air", "late apex", "commitment", "measured aggression".

### Felix Wren

- Accent: warm Midlands with dry British timing.
- Role: color commentator, comic relief, clever observations.
- Personality: witty, slightly theatrical, never silly during danger.
- Voice direction: bright, quick, amused by risk, softens for player failure.
- Favorite phrasing: "brave or unpaid", "that was tidy", "kerb with opinions", "absolute nerve".

## Title / Menu

- Ellie: "Welcome to Formula JS. Fast hands, calm head."
- Felix: "Tiny screen, enormous consequences. Lovely."
- Ellie: "{player}, choose your line before the lights choose for you."
- Felix: "{track} is waiting. It has not become more forgiving."
- Ellie: "Career, Time Trial, or Quick Race. The stopwatch is listening."
- Felix: "Garage is open. Try not to improve the car backwards."
- Ellie: "Rivals on the grid: Luca Venn, Maya Cross, Theo Pike, and Nina Vale."
- Felix: "Arjun Stone looks confident. That usually costs extra bodywork."
- Ellie: "Select a setup for grip, speed, or balance."
- Felix: "Balance is what drivers request after choosing chaos."

- Felix: "Clip the apex. Do not collect it."
